From the back cover…

From the dark echoes of skate parks, back alleys and derelict lots comes a young voice rising against the straightjacket of conformity. BLOCK gathers poems written in the pulse of the 1990s and early 2000s—where hip-hop meets punk rock, faith clashes with doubt, and creativity smashes its shape from concrete.

Eighty raw poems of survival and roasting the establishment: snapshots of youth caught between rebellion and reflection, belonging and exile, rhythm and rhyme. They chronicle the definition of self in a world that offers little more than mainstream labels.

Part memoir, part manifesto, this collection invites readers into the turbulence of identity and the forging of a voice—an invitation to give the struggle its flowers and recognize the streets are never satisfied with anything less than blood.
